"The Butcher, the Baker and the... other one": Shops in MUDs

Urgit writes about the variety of shops available. A list of shops at the bottom may be helpful for builders.

Good shops can really add value to an area. Yet too many Builders on most MUDs waste this opportunity to make their area unique and distinctive. They throw in a butcher, a baker, a weapons shop, an armour shop, a tavern and call it done. Many areas don't even need shops- have you ever seen a baker with a stall in a maze of underground caverns? - but people add them anyway. However, the worse case is where an area had the space and potential for several interesting shops, yet the Builder chose to stick to those that are in every other similar area in every other MUD.

People want their areas to be noticeable, to be visited, to be remembered. An imaginative approach to shops is one of many ways to help achieve that.

- Some areas will have more than one of some types of shop. A Cherek town will probably have far more than a single tavern, for example.

- Related shops. Those Cherek taverns don't exist is a vacuum. The beer may come from different brewes, who themselves will get ingredients from farmers and others. The barrels must be supplied by someone, and so on.

- If a nation has a specialty, it's likely there'll be more than one of that particular shop in the settlement. A Nadrak town will likely have more than one tanner. In Tolnedra, where commerce is so rampant and both class and competition strong forces, there may be a few of any type of shop. If there's a bakery, there might well be another merchant posing as a "High Class Baker", with items named and priced accordingly. If merchants are competing, think about how they might try to attract people to buy their own wares.

- Don't be afraid of having mobs selling useless items, or mixing up useful things with less worthwhile ones. More items available in shops helps add character and
texture to the area.

- Items bought in shops might not be finished products, but able to be turned into something useful elsewhere in the area. A shopkeepers might sell damaged armour for low prices that wouldn't be any real use, but perhaps another mob in the area, give suitable progs, could be able to turn those items into something possible to
wear. While this type of thing probably wouldn't constitute a miniquest, allowing you to make the equipment stronger than the points system usually allows, it adds
texture.

- However, shops can be made a part of good miniquests. For example, you might be rewarded for handing a letter to a Tolnedran nobleman in the middle of a party. However, the party dress code is typically strict, and you need to collect and wear certain items to be admitted. Several items can be bought from shops in the city, but one isn't. Yet you can buy the raw materials from a clothmaker and a haberdasher, and then give them to a mob with suitable progs who will create the required item. So many miniquests can be created in which shops can play an active and useful part.
