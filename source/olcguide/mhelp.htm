? act (Mobile Actions)

- sentinel: mob will not move around in the area, instead staying in one room.
- scavenger: random chance the mob will pick something up off of the floor in a room.
- banker: will deposit and withdraw money for characters.
- aggressive: will attack PC's according to its level.
- stay_area: mob will not wander out of its prescribed area.
- wimpy: mob will flee when it reaches a certain hp percentage.
- pet: mob is charmed, and someone's pet.
- train: mob can train other players and their stats.
- practice: mob helps characters practice skills.
- horse: mountable, but without mount code yet, we can't use it, yet :(
- quest: mob can be used as a quest target.
- undead: mob acts as a skeleton, ghost, or other undead, in spells and attacks.
- monk: powerful hand to hand warrior
- cleric: healer or hurter, based on how you want it to act toward you.
- mage: can cast mage spells
- thief: backstabs, steals, and runs away
- warrior: fights as a warrior, strong, many attacks, no magic
- noalign: mob not considered for alignment purposes, use sparingly.
- nopurge: mob may not be purged by an imm from the room.
- indoors: mob can't go outside to play
- outdoors: mob can't come in from the cold.
- healer: heals characters, for a certain fee.
- gain: characters may gain skills at this person.
- update_always: used with code updates, buggy, please don't use.
- changer: mob can exchange gold and silver for players.

? affect (mobile affect flags)

- blind: mob is blinded
- invisible: mob is affected by the invisible spell.
- detect_evil: can sense evil beings
- detect_invis: mob can see invisible people
- detect_magic: mob can sense magical items
- detect_hidden: mob can see hidden or sneaking people
- detect_good: mob can sense the light side of the Force.
- sneak: mob is sneaking around, hard to see him come in or leave.
- sanctuary: mob is surrounded by an aura, taking only half normal damage.
- faerie_fire: mobs Armor Class is lowered based on level
- infared: mob can sense heat in the dark.
- curse: mob is cursed, making it a little easier to hit with weapons and spells.
- poison: mob is poisoned, and will lose some hp during the course of a fight
- protect_evil: bonus save vs. spell and AC against evil characters
- protect_good: bonus save vs. spell and AC against good characters
- hide: mob is affected by hide. Need detect hidden to see it.
- sleep: mob has been put to sleep
- charm: mob has been charmed and is now under the control of someone else.
- flying: mob is affected by the flying spell.
- pass_door: mob is affected by pass door, affecting its movement and ability to be bashed.
- haste: mob is affected by haste, increasing the number of its attacks.
- calm: mob less likely to be aggressive and also may hit less.
- plague: mob is affected by plague, which reduces hp regeneration. Contagious.
- weaken: mob is affected by weaken, and its strength is reduced
- dark_vision: mob can see in the dark.
- berserk: mob is in a frenzy, affecting its hit and damage by a marginal amount.
- swim: mob can move through watery terrain.
- regeneration: mob would gain hitpoints back faster when healing, but is bugged. Don't use.
- slow: mob has had its speed reduced to less than normal, will hit less often.

? form (mobile form flags)

- edible: parts can be eaten after killed
- poison: parts poisonous when eaten
- magical: creature is not "natural", and made of magic stuff
- construct: creature is a magical construct, such as a golem
- instant_decay: mob corpse rots upon death
- other: mob form cannot be easily classified
- animal: mob is a basic, natural animal
- sentient: mob is capable of complex thoughts, human-like
- undead: a spirit of the nether realms, skeleton, ghost, etc.
- mist: form is that of a cloud or fine mist
- intangible: cannot be seen or touched or thought of
- biped: walks on two legs
- centaur: body of a horse, torso and head and arms of a human
- insect: a bug, generally small
- spider: 8 legs, feared by many people
- crustacean: crabs or other shellfish
- worm: mush, creepy crawly insect that moves around in dirt.
- blob: mob is a blob, without bones, slimy, oozy
- mammal: warm blooded
- bird: has wings to fly, generally small
- reptile: cold blooded, scales, forked tongue
- snake: slithery, cold blooded, feetless reptiles
- dragon: very large and fearsome beast
- amphibian: salamander, or any other creature that can live both on land and in water
- fish: fins, scales, lives in water and nowhere else
- cold_blood: cold blooded creature, needs sunlight, affected heavily by cold weather

? imm/res/vuln

Different types of immunities, resistances, and vulnerabilities are listed here. Most are self-explanatory, but if you need help, ask an area imp to explain them for you.

? off (mobile offenseive behaviors)

- area_attack: mob can hit more than one person at a time
- backstab: mob is capable of using backstab skill against characters
- bash: mob may bash character
- berserk: mob may use berserk skill during combat
- disarm: mob will sometimes disarm during combat
- dodge: mob has the dodge skill
- fade: mob can fade in and out, hard to hit or see during a fight
- fast: mob may take the initiative, getting the first attack in a fight
- kick: mob throws out a mildly damaging kick during the fight
- dirt_kick: blinds opponents temporarily.
- parry: mob can fend off weapons with his own
- rescue: mob will rescue those it deems worthy of it.
- tail: mob uses tail for an extra weapon in a fight
- trip: mob can take someone's feet out from under them
- crush: overpowering attack, extra damage
- assist_all: mob will assist anyone on a whim
- assist_align: mob will assist like-aligned players and mobiles in a fight
- assist_race: mob will assist players and mobs of the same race
- assist_players: mob will assist players that are in a fight
- assist_guard: mob will aid guards in seeing to justice
- assist_vnum: mob assists only mobs of the same vnum
- flurry: executes a flurry of attacks at random
- spellbane: capable of reflecting spells back at the caster
- riposte: a counterattack, based on parry success

? size (mobile sizes)

Sizes affect how hard and how difficult it is to hit certain creatures. Large creatures may be a little slower, but hit harder, where as small creatures might not do much damage, but are fast and hard to hit. Keep mobile sizes relative and appropriate, humans being medium.

? spec (special mobile functions)

- spec_breath_any: mob will cast any type of breath spell
- spec_breath_gas: mob casts gas breath
- spec_breath_acid: mob casts acid breath
- spec_breath_fire: mob casts fire breath
- spec_breath_frost: mob casts frost breath
- spec_breath_lighnting: mob casts lightning breath
- spec_cast_adept: mob casts adept-level mage spells.
- spec_cast_cleric: mob casts cleric spells, like blind.
- spec_cast_judge: mob casts judging spells (not sure what they do)
- spec_cast_mage: casts mage spells, like fireball.
- spec_cast_undead: casts plaguing, undead spells
- spec_executioner: mob acts as a form of executioner of the law.
- spec_fido: mob will devour corpses on the ground.
- spec_guard: mob enforces the law, protecting the innocent and punishing those needing it.
- spec_janitor: mob picks up things from the ground, as its cleaning duty.
- spec_mayor: DON'T USE - specific movement of Sendar's mayor, won't work in your town.
- spec_poison: mob will poison player, if given the chance
- spec_thief: mob backstabs and steals money from a player.
- spec_nasty: low down, dirty scoundrel.
- spec_troll_member and spec_ogre_member: no such things as trolls and ogres in Prophecy. Don't use.
- spec_patrolman: an old police officer, please don't use.
- spec_angarak_guard: an Eastern guard, will react unfavorably towards Alorns.
- spec_alorn_guard: A western guard, will attack Angaraks on sight.
- spec_questmaster: holds questmaster's routine and code, ask imps before using.
- spec_inkeeper: hostel keeper's code, don't use

? wear-loc (mobile wear locations)

Most of the wear locations are self-explanatory, and the same as "? wear" above.

- rfinger: right finger
- lfinger: left finger
- rwrist: right wrist
- lwrist: left wrist
- neck1/2: first or second neck location
- none: in mobile's inventory
