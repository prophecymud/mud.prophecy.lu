? act (Mobile Actions)

- sentinel: mob will not move around in the area, instead staying in one room.
- scavenger: random chance the mob will pick something up off of the floor in a room.
- banker: will deposit and withdraw money for characters.
- aggressive: will attack PC's according to its level.
- stay_area: mob will not wander out of its prescribed area.
- wimpy: mob will flee when it reaches a certain hp percentage.
- pet: mob is charmed, and someone's pet.
- train: mob can train other players and their stats.
- practice: mob helps characters practice skills.
- horse: mountable, but without mount code yet, we can't use it, yet :(
- quest: mob can be used as a quest target.
- undead: mob acts as a skeleton, ghost, or other undead, in spells and attacks.
- monk: powerful hand to hand warrior
- cleric: healer or hurter, based on how you want it to act toward you.
- mage: can cast mage spells
- thief: backstabs, steals, and runs away
- warrior: fights as a warrior, strong, many attacks, no magic
- noalign: mob not considered for alignment purposes, use sparingly.
- nopurge: mob may not be purged by an imm from the room.
- indoors: mob can't go outside to play
- outdoors: mob can't come in from the cold.
- healer: heals characters, for a certain fee.
- gain: characters may gain skills at this person.
- update_always: used with code updates, buggy, please don't use.
- changer: mob can exchange gold and silver for players.

? affect (mobile affect flags)

- blind: mob is blinded
- invisible: mob is affected by the invisible spell.
- detect_evil: can sense evil beings
- detect_invis: mob can see invisible people
- detect_magic: mob can sense magical items
- detect_hidden: mob can see hidden or sneaking people
- detect_good: mob can sense the light side of the Force.
- sneak: mob is sneaking around, hard to see him come in or leave.
- sanctuary: mob is surrounded by an aura, taking only half normal damage.
- faerie_fire: mobs Armor Class is lowered based on level
- infared: mob can sense heat in the dark.
- curse: mob is cursed, making it a little easier to hit with weapons and spells.
- poison: mob is poisoned, and will lose some hp during the course of a fight
- protect_evil: bonus save vs. spell and AC against evil characters
- protect_good: bonus save vs. spell and AC against good characters
- hide: mob is affected by hide. Need detect hidden to see it.
- sleep: mob has been put to sleep
- charm: mob has been charmed and is now under the control of someone else.
- flying: mob is affected by the flying spell.
- pass_door: mob is affected by pass door, affecting its movement and ability to be bashed.
- haste: mob is affected by haste, increasing the number of its attacks.
- calm: mob less likely to be aggressive and also may hit less.
- plague: mob is affected by plague, which reduces hp regeneration. Contagious.
- weaken: mob is affected by weaken, and its strength is reduced
- dark_vision: mob can see in the dark.
- berserk: mob is in a frenzy, affecting its hit and damage by a marginal amount.
- swim: mob can move through watery terrain.
- regeneration: mob would gain hitpoints back faster when healing, but is bugged. Don't use.
- slow: mob has had its speed reduced to less than normal, will hit less often.

? apptype (object addapply types)

- affects: do not use this any more, to add permspells please use addaffect
- object: affects how an object might work
- immune/resist/vuln: “addapply immune/res/vuln [stat] [modifier] [type of i/r/v] [level]” Check with area imps before adding immunities or resistances. Remember, adding immunities or resistances to objects is forbidden.
- weapon: affects the actions of a weapon

? area

- changed: an automatic flag when someone edits anything inside the area.
- added: an area that has been recently added to the mud.
- noquest: no autoquests will be given in the area with this flag.
- noenter: allows nobody but the builder to enter the area.
- newbie: an area that only "newbies" (levels 1-5) can enter, like the mud school. Should not really be used.
- norecall: an area generally made no-recall for purposes of the recall skill, “word of recall” spell, or clan recall.

? container (container flags)

- closeable: container can be closed.
- pickproof: thieves can't pick the lock on the container
- closed: flagged as closed upon repop
- locked: locks a closed container on repop
- puton: objects are put on, rather than in, this container

? exit

- door: creates a door in a specified direction.
- closed: sets a door to reset at the closed position.
- locked: locks a closed door, in a specified direction.
- pickproof: thieves can't pick the lock on the door.
- nopass: pass_door will not work trying to go through the door.
- easy: thieves find it easy to pick this kind of lock.
- hard: thieves find picking this lock a small challenge.
- infuriating: thieves will be yelling and screaming to get this open.
- noclose: Characters won't be able to shut the door
- nolock: Characters won't be able to lock the door.
- hidden: hides the exit, as long as there's no door.
- window: makes a window to look through, as long as there isn't a door already there.

? exra (object extra flags)

- glow: The object is glowing with its own light, a light source that can be seen in the dark.
- hum: Object is humming with energy, and can be heard in a quiet room.
- dark: object is hidden, and is darkened to someone who cannot see hidden things.
- evil: Object reeks of evil, and will have a red aura to it.
- invis: object is invisible, and the detect invis spell is needed to see it.
- magic: object has a magical quality about it, and will have magical aura to it.
- nodrop: character cannot drop the object from his inventory, unless he removes curse from it.
- bless: object is blessed, and will do well against evil or unholy creatures.
- anti-good: persons of good align (1000 to 300) may not use this item.
- anti-neutral: persons of neutral align (300 to -300) may not use this item.
- anti-evil: persons of evil align (-300 to -1000) may not use this item.
- noremove: if this object is worn, character cannot remove it unless remove curse is cast.
- inventory: for a shop keeper. Sets the object to repop indefinitely to a shop.
- nopurge: object may not be purged by an imm from the room.
- rotdeath: will receive a timer when a character dies, and when that timer expires, the object will crumble and be lost.
- visdeath: will not be seen until the character has killed the mobile that has the object.
- nosac: object may not be sacrificed to the character's god.
- nonmetal: object has non-metallic properties, that could be harmful to certain mobiles.
- nolocate: cannot be found by the locate object spell
- meltdrop: will dissolve into smoke when the character drops it.
- timer and sell: not to be used.
- burnproof: the object cannot be damaged or destroyed by magic/weapons
- nouncurse: object may not be uncursed using the remove curse spell
- pkonly: Player Killers may only hold or use this item
- nopkonly: Non-player killers may only hold or use this item.

? form (mobile form flags)

- edible: parts can be eaten after killed
- poison: parts poisonous when eaten
- magical: creature is not "natural", and made of magic stuff
- construct: creature is a magical construct, such as a golem
- instant_decay: mob corpse rots upon death
- other: mob form cannot be easily classified
- animal: mob is a basic, natural animal
- sentient: mob is capable of complex thoughts, human-like
- undead: a spirit of the nether realms, skeleton, ghost, etc.
- mist: form is that of a cloud or fine mist
- intangible: cannot be seen or touched or thought of
- biped: walks on two legs
- centaur: body of a horse, torso and head and arms of a human
- insect: a bug, generally small
- spider: 8 legs, feared by many people
- crustacean: crabs or other shellfish
- worm: mush, creepy crawly insect that moves around in dirt.
- blob: mob is a blob, without bones, slimy, oozy
- mammal: warm blooded
- bird: has wings to fly, generally small
- reptile: cold blooded, scales, forked tongue
- snake: slithery, cold blooded, feetless reptiles
- dragon: very large and fearsome beast
- amphibian: salamander, or any other creature that can live both on land and in water
- fish: fins, scales, lives in water and nowhere else
- cold_blood: cold blooded creature, needs sunlight, affected heavily by cold weather

? imm/res/vuln

Different types of immunities, resistances, and vulnerabilities are listed here. Most are self-explanatory, but if you need help, ask an area imp to explain them for you.

? liquid (liquid types)

This will list a series of liquids you may use in fountains and drink containers. There are differing columns with different stats.

- name: name of the liquid in question
- color: the color it will appear when someone looks at the container
- proof: the higher the number, the quicker and longer the person stays drunk after drinking it
- full: number of hours it will satisfy hunger. Some are none (like water) because they have no value in the way of nutrition.
- thirst: number of hours it will satisfy thirst
- food: its value as a food
- ssize: how much of it will fit in a container, or how many drinks you could get out of filling once

- area_attack: mob can hit more than one person at a time
- backstab: mob is capable of using backstab skill against characters
- bash: mob may bash character
- berserk: mob may use berserk skill during combat
- disarm: mob will sometimes disarm during combat

- spellbane: capable of reflecting spells back at the caster

? room

- dark: players without lights will not be able to see, unless it is outdoors and in broad daylight hours.
- no_mob: mobiles will not wander/flee into the room, good for healers or rooms frequented by players.
- indoors: changes in time and weather cannot be seen by the player if the room is flagged indoors.
- no_teleport: players may not use the teleport spell to enter this room.
- no_clanrecall: clan members may not return to their clan halls from here.
- no_quit: players may not quit in this room, and are asked to go before they do.
- private: only 2 people or mobiles may be in the room at any one given time.
- safe: fighting will not occur in this room as long as it is safe.
- solitary: only one person or mob may be in this room at a given time.
- pet_shop: flags the room as a pet shop, using that room and the next room consecutively in vnums. The first room is where the shopkeeper sells the pets, and the second room is where the pet mobs will be reset.
- no_recall: flags the room unable to be recalled from, using the recall skill or the word of recall spell.
- imp_only: implementer immortals may enter this room only.
- gods_only: immortals only in this room.
- heroes_only: mortal heroes may enter this room, or higher level.
- newbies_only: characters from level 1 to 5 may enter this room only.
- law: Pk rules will be enforced in this room, useful for city streets.
- nowhere: someone in this room will not appear when someone types "where"
- wizard_only: another immortal level, for imms only in this room.
- stable: mount seller, and as code doesn't support mount, it has no use right now.
- no_magic: spells may not be cast in this room
- nogate: characters may not use the gate spell to enter this room.
- nologon: if a character quits here, they will log back on at recall.
- nostatus: characters' hunger and thirst will not change in this room.

? sector

- inside: 1 movement point to move into this sector.
- city: 2 movement points to move into this sector.
- field: 2 movement points to move into this sector.
- forest: 3 movement points to move into this sector.
- hills: 4 movement points to move into this sector.
- mountain: 6 movement points to move into this sector.
- swim: 4 movement points to move into this sector.
- noswim: fly spell required to move over this terrain, or a boat.
- unused: unused, and leave it that way (don't use if for a sector).
- air: fly spell required to negotiate through this region.
- desert: 9 mv points, and will affect thirst a little quicker.

? size (mobile sizes)

Sizes affect how hard and how difficult it is to hit certain creatures. Large creatures may be a little slower, but hit harder, where as small creatures might not do much damage, but are fast and hard to hit. Keep mobile sizes relative and appropriate, humans being medium.

? wear (object wear flags)

- take: allows the character to get the object off of the ground.
- finger: rings and such.
- neck: capes, necklaces, and such.
- body: worn about the torso
- head: helmets, worn on the head.
- legs: pants, leggings, arm plates, anything worn on the legs.
- feet: boots, shoes, socks, anything worn on the feet.
- hands: gloves, gauntlets, anything worn on the hands.
- arms: arm plates, sleeves, anything worn on the arms.
- shield: anything that could be used as a shield against attack.
- about: worn about the body, like cloaks.
- waist: belts, anything worn about the waist.
- wrist: bracelets and bracers, anything worn about the wrists.
- wield: weapons, anything wielded to do harm against another.
- hold: wands, staves, anything held in the hand
- float: set to float next to the character, and will fall away from the corpse upon death.

- lwrist: left wrist

? wtype (special weapon flags)

- flaming: causes extra damage by burning the victim, possibility of blinding by smoke
- frost: extra damage by freezing cold, may also add chill touch affect
- vampiric: draws some life from the victim, adding to the attacker, also lowers align.
- sharp: the weapon will do a small amount of random extra damage
- vorpal: has a random chance of doing double damage
- twohands: requires two hands to wield and use
- shocking: causes extra damage thorugh electrocution, and may weaken victims
- poison: may envenom the blood of an opponent.
